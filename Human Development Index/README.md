# Human Development Index — Global Analysis and Dashboard

Multi-dimensional analysis of the United Nations Human Development Index (HDI) across 190+ countries — visualizing the interplay between education, life expectancy, income, and development tier using Tableau, with a written research report and presentation.

**Tools:** Tableau · CSV · Excel · PowerPoint

---

## Overview

The Human Development Index (HDI) is the UN's composite measure of national achievement across three dimensions: long and healthy life (life expectancy), knowledge (education index), and a decent standard of living (GNI per capita). This project analyzes HDI data to surface global development patterns, regional disparities, and country-level trajectories.

## Data Coverage
- 190+ countries
- Indicators: HDI score, Life Expectancy Index, Education Index, GNI Index, HDI rank, HDI tier (Very High / High / Medium / Low)
- Time series data enabling trend analysis across development periods

## Key Analysis

**Global HDI Distribution**
Mapped HDI scores across all countries using a choropleth — revealing the stark development divide between Sub-Saharan Africa (predominantly Low/Medium tier) and Europe/North America (Very High tier).

**Regional Comparison**
Compared median HDI scores across 7 UN regions — East Asia and Pacific, Europe and Central Asia, Latin America and Caribbean, Middle East and North Africa, South Asia, Sub-Saharan Africa, North America.

**HDI Component Breakdown**
Decomposed HDI into its three sub-indices by country tier — revealing which components are strongest and weakest within each development tier.

**High Performers vs. Underperformers**
Identified countries that outperform their income level on human development and countries that underperform relative to GDP — surfacing policy-relevant outliers.

**Development Trajectory**
Tracked HDI score changes over time by region — showing accelerating development in South Asia and East Africa vs. stagnation in conflict-affected regions.

## Key Findings
- Norway, Switzerland, and Iceland consistently rank in the top 5 driven by near-perfect Education Index scores
- Sub-Saharan Africa has the widest within-region HDI variance (Seychelles 0.79 vs. South Sudan 0.39)
- Life expectancy is the strongest HDI predictor for Low-tier countries; GNI per capita dominates at High/Very High tiers
- 18 countries improved HDI tier between 2015-2022, all in South/Southeast Asia or East Africa
